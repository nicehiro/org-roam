#+title: OSCAR: Omni-Embodiment Action-Conditioned World Model for Robotics
#+filetags: :WM:


OSCAR for Robotics proposes an action-conditioned video world model for evaluating robot policies across different embodiments. The key idea is to represent actions as rendered 2D kinematic skeletons, providing a unified conditioning signal that works for multiple robot arms, mobile manipulators, and even human hands, instead of relying on embodiment-specific joint spaces or end-effector poses. The authors also build a large curated training pipeline that filters and deduplicates diverse robot and egocentric human manipulation datasets, then finetune Cosmos-Predict2.5-2B to generate future robot videos from an initial frame and action sequence.

-----


Input: first RGB frame + rendered 2D action skeleton sequence
Backbone: VAE-encoded video diffusion transformer, finetuned from Cosmos-Predict2.5-2B
Conditioning: skeleton latent tokens added to noisy video latent tokens
Output: future robot video rollout following the given action sequence
